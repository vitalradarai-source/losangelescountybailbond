Bail 101

How Do Bail Bonds Work in California?

When someone is arrested, a judge sets a bail amount based on the charges, criminal history, and flight risk. Most families cannot pay the full amount in cash — that's where a bail bondsman comes in.

You pay Angels Bail Bonds 10% of the total bail amount (regulated by California Insurance Code § 1800.4). We post the full bond, and your loved one is released. The 10% is our fee — not a deposit.
